Gnome Asia Summit 2013 in Seoul

2013-05-30

I attended the Gnome Asia Summit 2013, held in Seoul, the capital of South Korea. It’s the first time I’ve been abroad, and so many things are new to me. Thanks for the Gnome Foundation to offer this chance for us, to meet many Gnome fans in Seoul.

The two-day summit began with the welcome speech by the local team. I was not familiar with the venue and spent some time reaching there. When I arrived, the speech was just over. I was in time for the keynote speech from Karen Sandler. She told her own story about meeting gnome. I mentioned that she was brave to tell us that she has a heart disease and have to use a pace-maker. She talked about the security problem of commercial medical devices, in that their source code are not open, and FDA won’t check the code for security problems. I’m working on a medical system too in my lab, therefore these information are very impressive to me.

Then I listened to a few talks, for various topics and different talking styles. Allan Day talked about some basic approaches to contribute to Gnome, which I think is very useful. I’m not familiar to Gnome develop, so topics like Streamer, Sandbox etc. are not very interesting. I like the talk on Tizen development, cause I’ve been tracing the development of Meego. I’m glad this system is evolving in its way into Tizen.

I attended the talks by our Chinese members. Bin Lin talked about the basics on developing Gnome shell plugins. Very interesting talk, with step by step instructions. Max talked about the Ezilla system developed for Education. Wonderful work, just by a small team. Yu Liansu showed how she use Linux and free software to do web designs. I was informed that the artwork of the summit including the post and banner by her. Great work, and herself is admirable.

I held a training course on Gnome translation. Translation is not that important as develop, but it’s an easier way the students could contribute. I was glad that many students attended the training. I talked about how I got to know Linux, and how I became a translator. I introduced the basics of i18n and l10n, and showed them how to begin Gnome translation on Damned Lies. Then I explained some specific problems in translation, with a few examples. Hope my talk will provide a start-up guide for the newbies. My slides could be downloaded from here.

From the summit I got to know many people, and made many friends. I find Max very active, and full of energy. The summit is very successful thanks to him, and other Gnome Asia members’ effort. Many Korean members are very kind. They helped us a lot in how to take a subway, buy a SIM card and so on. Thanks to them.

And thanks to Gnome foundation for sponsoring my trip. Thanks to our Chinese members for their help in Visa application, travelling and slides preparation.

Some photos taken in Seoul. This is a beautiful city. Love the barbecue!
